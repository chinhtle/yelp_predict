Cafeteria  

Categories: American (New), Southern, Breakfast & Brunch, Diners
Neighborhood: Chelsea

5/11/2008
I'm DEFINITELY coming back here for the truffle-oil mac and cheese.  

The place has such a swanky atmosphere, which I totally dug.  I was super suprised to see chicken and waffles on their menu... mainly cuz the only other place I've been to that serves that is Roscoe's in LA... and I definitely don't go there for its "swankiness".  

Minus 1 star cuz the maple syrup was too watered-down and tasted like it was some sort of "lite" version.. and I despise anything light/low fat/ or diet...  I mean come on... if you're gonna eat chicken 'n waffles, you gotta go all the way w/ the fatty-tasty-goodness.

Red Mango - CLOSED 

Category: Ice Cream & Frozen Yogurt

5/11/2008
RED MANGO'S COMING TO THE BAY AREA???... HALLELUJA!  

Thanks for the info, people.  I had been suffering from PinkBerry withdrawals ever since I tried it for the 1st time in NYC back in October.  Then a few weeks ago, I was introduced to Red Mango... good god that stuff is scrumptious.  It's got less of a tangy aftertaste which I love cuz I don't get sick of it as fast as I do w/ PinkBerry.  I also like how they have more topping selections ... like watermelon and tangerines.  If you haven't had the yogurt w/ watermelons, it's very refreshing and I totally recommend it.  My fav combo is: watermelon + mochi  and captain crunch.  Try it... you'll like it.  =)

----------- UPDATE --------------
5/15/2008
I just scarffed down a LARGE original yogurt w/ strawberries, captain crunch, and coco pebbles all by my lonesome.  Holy Moly, it was oral-gasmic.  FYI... They don't have mochi available yet.

Boboquivari's  

Categories: Steakhouses, Seafood
Neighborhood: Marina/Cow Hollow

5/6/2008
Compared to Alexanders in San Jose, where I had the best porterhouse I've ever tasted, Bobo's failed slightly in comparison.  But what made up for it was their side of sauteed mushrooms that made anything we dipped in it (especially the steak) taste like heaven.  I liked that you could order 1/2 of a roasted crab, but flavor wise, it was no Crustaceans.  The bartender made an aweful grey goose and tonic... my hubby and I are still wondering how someone could mess that one up... I mean... the ingredients are listed in the name for goodness sake.  So if you want a cocktail, I'd suggest sticking to what they already have on their drink menu.

Request sitting at  a table by the window on the 3rd floor, and I'd recommend reservations... even on a weeknight.  Also, if you mention that it's your anniversary or other special occassion, they'll bring you a  free dessert!
