Yatai Asian Tapas Bar  

Categories: Japanese, Asian Fusion, Sushi Bars
Neighborhood: Hollywood Hills West

4/28/2009
Yatai is a 4.5er for me!  I'm a total spanish-tapas kinda girl, so asian-tapas fits right in there to my liking.  I'll be so happy if I successfully yelp a place like this up in the Bay.  Came here on a friday night right before heading to a show @ The Comedy Store down the street.  I will definitely, definitely, definitely come back for more of these items that I totally consider to be 5-star worthy:

- Crispy Shrimp Toast: oh my god, oh my god, oh my god!  I have yet to see this item on any other asian restaurant menu, so this I highly recommend.  In order to satisfy my cravings as I am miles and miles away, I found a receipe on youtube and will attemp to replicate these myself this weekend. no joke.  they're THAT good and THAT addictive.

- Spicy Tuna Tar Tar w/ Crispy Won Ton Chips: I LOVE that they pile on a heaping mound of tuna on each chip.  A must if you are a lover of ahi tuna like myself.

- Sauteed Green Beans: I know, I know, it's just green beans, right?  what's the big deal?  I dunno, they're just hella good.  Just the right amount of crunch and drizzled w/ the perfect sauce.  Deeee-lectable!  

- Grilled Eel Rice Bowl: Always one of my fav items on a menu & I LOVED that it was only $9. They give you a good amount too.

Here are the other items I tasted that night.  They didn't reach 5-star status in my book, but they were okay.  Edible, tasty, good fillers if you need them, but not the best I've ever had:

- Samosas
- Orange Chicken
- Fried Chicken
- Octopus Potato Balls
- Baked Black Cod

Norma's  

Categories: Breakfast & Brunch, American (New)
Neighborhood: Midtown West

2/12/2009
Eating @ Norma's for Restaurant Week was a total no-brainer for me.  For $24, it was like having 3 full entrees.  SO MUCH FOOD.  I highly recommend all 3 of my choices.  

1st course: I had the Norma's Eggs Benedict.  Normally, I don't order eggs benedict... something about the tangy hollandaise sauce and english muffins that I'm not a huge fan of.  But Norma's version is served on a pancake and the sauce is quite different from the hollandaise sauce that I'm use to.  It was smoother, creamier, lighter... and just better!

2nd course: I had the Gone in 60 Seconds Steak.  Again, loved the sauce poured on top of it.  And the portions were pretty big too.  In fact, I was already pretty full after my 1st course, that I took a good 3/4 of my steak to go.  I had it for dinner that night and it was still just as good.

3rd course: Chocolate Decadence French Toast topped w/ strawberries, pistachios & chocolate sauce.  Crazy, right??

Outside of restaurant week, each of these meals cost about $20/each... so I really can't give anything less than 5 stars for this being such a GREAT DEAL.

Hunan Home's Restaurant  

Category: Chinese

12/7/2008
Let me start by saying that this 5 star review is for my favorite carb items on their menu.  I LOVE CARBS.  Yea I said it.  

My new hands-down all-time favorite place to order fried rice is Hunan Homes.  Actually, that's not true.  Prior to today, their combination fried rice was always what I'd crave whenever I'm in the mood for chinese food.  If I'm feeling under the weather, I'd ask the hubby to pick some up for me... I think it's like my chicken soup, cuz I always feel better afterwards.   Theirs is just so full of flavor and super moist.  (I really hate dry/bland fried rice, where the rice and other items don't stick together.  I'd much rather have plain white rice if that were the case.)  So this place is my fried rice heaven.... yea I know I sound like I'm going overboard.  But I told you I love carbs.

TODAY, however... today I discovered an even tastier fried rice... their Fujian Fried Rice!  It's ridiculous how good it is.  I mean, it's just rice for goodness sake, right?  Wrong my friend.  It's like a meal in itself.  Their menu describes it as "fried rice topped w/ shrimp and scallops in gravy".  GRAVY PEOPLE!  OKAY???  What are you waiting for..... pick up an order to go and thank me later.

My other carb item that I love is green onion pancakes, and I am so happy that this place has them... although it's not on their menu, and it's only available during lunch on the weekends, I believe.  Hecka weird, but if that's when I need to have it in order to get me carb-fix on, then I'll take it.  Try dipping them in white vinegar!

Great America's Halloween Haunt  

Category: Arts & Entertainment

10/26/2008
I enjoyed Great America's Halloween Haunt.  

If you show your student ID, you can get in for $29.99!  We went last Saturday, and although there were lines for pretty much ALL of the haunted mazes, they moved faster than I thought they would.  Surprisingly, there were NO LINES for any of the rides,  (on a Saturday night, even!), so you can go on as many as you want for however many times that you want! Getting my Drop Zone, Top Gun, and Deamon fix was totally worth 30 bucks.  Oh, and don't forget to get your corndog+fries, churro, and funnel cake fix on while you're there too!

BTW, parking's $10.  And remember to dress warmly & wear comfy shoes.  

HAPPY HALLOWEEN EVERYONE!  =)
