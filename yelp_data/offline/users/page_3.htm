    Bizou Bizou por favor... and keep 'em comin'!  

    I discovered my new favorite martini here at Left Bank last night.  Bizou means "kiss" and it's actually more like a margarita that's loaded with TONS of fresh watermelon.  SO GOOD.  I will definitely be back for more of that stuff.... mark my words.  

    As for the food, we ordered the mussels, side of fries, risotto, and cheese fondue.   Everything was tasty... my only complaint is that the bread they brought out w/ the fondue was rock hard.  It didn't really bother me though because I figured out a plan B.... use the pommes frites instead and dip those yummy suckers into the molten brie, bleu, and goat cheese trifecta of fondue gooeyness.  

    We also sat outside which was fun for people-watching and great conversations.

Cascal  

Categories: Tapas Bars, Spanish

9/29/2008
LOVE LOVE LOVE their red sangrias.  Hands down, this place has the absolute BEST sangrias I've ever tasted... and yes, I do order sangrias whenever I see it on a menu.  So that's saying quite a lot.  You'll often find me at the bar making a sangria pit-stop before a night out on the town.  Yep, they're that good!

BUT I HATE HATE HATE their tapas.... We once ordered the tapas sampler cuz I figured we'd find at least one winner in the bunch... but me 'n the hubby were disappointed with every single item on the plate.  So I'm still looking for a good tapas place in the neighborhood.  Until then, I'll have to go to the Mission district whenever I've got a tapas craving.  

5 stars for their addictive red sangrias.
1 star for the sub-par food.
= 3 stars overall for me.

Geta  

Categories: Japanese, Sushi Bars
Neighborhoods: Piedmont Ave, North Oakland

9/29/2008
Let me start by saying that I'm a BIG unagi lover.  If I could, I'd eat unagi-don (bbq eel over rice) everytime I go to a Japanese restaurant... but I usually refrain from that cuz it's always on the more expensive side (~$14 on average I'd say).  

So I was instantly drawn to this place when I saw the line of people inside and the menu posted outside listing my precious unagi-don for ONLY $7.95!!  WHAT?? SEVEN NINETY FIVE!?!

Yes, this place is pretty dumpy inside, (so I just order to go) but what makes this a 5-star review is the HUMUNGOUS piece of eel they gave me.  I'm used to seeing 4 or 5 small pieces of eel over rice, like the ones that you get on sushi.  But Geta gives you a big mother slab of bbq eel the size of your head.  So not only is my fave item cheaper here, but the portion is bigger too?? HOLY BANANAS I was so surprised and oh-so-very happy.  

Sigh, that made my Wednesday.

Picaro  

Categories: Spanish, Basque, Tapas Bars
Neighborhood: Mission

9/2/2008
If I'm ever hungry and find myself in the Mission, my mind automatically thinks of  
(1) Picaro's tortilla de patatas and
(2)  a nice chilled glass (or better yet, a pitcher) of their sangria.  

They have a ton of other tasty tapas like fried calamari, garlic mushrooms, green beans sauteed in garlic, spicy potatoes, garlic shrimp, and chicken croquettes ...but lemme tell ya... those damn potato & onion omelets make me dance in my seat (literally) with every bite.  I also love how they serve their sangrias really really cold and w/o any ice cubes... cuz one thing I can't stomach is a watered down sangria.  Ya heard meh?!

Bi-Rite Creamery  

Category: Ice Cream & Frozen Yogurt
Neighborhood: Mission

9/1/2008
If this place ever gets rid of Ricanela (cinnamon ice cream that's LOADED w/ snickerdoodle cookie pieces), I will have a MAAAAY-JA meltdown.  

I know some people complain about the long line, but I don't mind it.  As long as I'm w/ good company, the line moves pretty fast and it helps build up my excitedness and anticipation for what I know is about to come.... INSANELY GOOD ICE CREAM.

True story: As it was finally my turn at the front of the line, the Bi Rite worker asked me what I'd like to order... I blurted out w/ enthusiasm, "One QUART of Ricanela, please."  A tad surprised by such a tall order, he asked, "Any thing else for you, ma'am?".  To which I responded still equally excited.... "And a VERY large spoon!!"  

Minus 1 star for the quart costing me $14 freakin' 99, which in turn prevents me from being able to afford such an indulgence on a more frequent basis.

TAO Beach  

Categories: Swimming Pools, Lounges
Neighborhood: Westside

7/21/2008
If you're staying at The Venetian or Palazzo, only then would I recommend checking out Tao Beach.  I came here around 11:30am on a Sat. afternoon after unsuccessfully searching for a free spot around the hotel's 7 or so other overly crowded pools.  There was no line and no cover, so my friends and I plopped our things next to the dj booth and hopped right in the pool.  I would've given this place more stars if the music was better, but it was just weird.  The music, vibe, and energy is much better at Bare... so if you're not already staying at The Venetian/Palazzo, I wouldn't recommed the trek over here.
