Rok Bistro  

Categories: American (New), Fondue

8/19/2011
Let me start by saying, I am not a big fan of fondue places... yea, the concept is fun and whimsical, especially if you're hanging out with girlfriends or on a first date... but for me, I usually end up being disappointed with the food & the price I paid for said food.  

That being said, Rok was pretty damn good!  It may be because I had low expectations.  Having a gc from restaurants.com didn't hurt either.  But everything I had tasted WAY better than La Fondue and every other fondue place I've been to.  I would order all of these again:

SPINACH AVOCADO SALAD -- I basically like anything with avocado, and appreciate the huge wedges that topped my plate.  No skimpin on the good stuff!  Me & the hubby both loved.

PESTO PARMESAN CHEESE FONDUE -  Very tasty!  Sometimes a cheese fondue can be well... too cheesy, and I end up getting sick of it half way through... but the hubby and I used every single piece of bread/vegetable/fruit to dip into that pot of gooey goodness.  When we ran out of stuff to dip with, I was tempted to ask if we could take the rest of the cheese home with us... but I refrained myself.  

JUMBO PRAWNS & SCALLOPS - LOVED LOVED LOVED!!  Again... they didn't skimp on the quality or SIZE of these suckers.  This is where they have their competitors BEAT... I remember getting some rinky dink pieces at La Fondue, and although they were good, there just wasn't enough of it.  I also enjoyed the ginger soy dipping sauce.      

FILET MIGNON - It melted like buttah in my mouth.  I think the key is knowing how to slice the meat properly before grilling it on the hot stone.  Cut against the grain, people!  

CHOCOLATE TRUFFLES FONDUE - The was actually pretty average. I thought it would have more kick with the carmel & salt, but it was pretty tame.  I definitely still enjoyed it though, and also liked the accompanying strawberries, rice krispy treats, pound cake & marshmallows covered in cocoa.  I thought the size of each one made for the perfect bite! Eh...  I'd probably try a different flavor next time.

I am now a converted fondue fan!!... or maybe just a Rok fan.  Thanks for a great date night!

Mission Ranch  

Categories: Venues & Event Spaces, American (New)

9/15/2010
I'm pretty sure I ate $30 worth of chocolate covered strawberries alone when my hubby and I ate here for Sunday Brunch 2 weekends ago.  

The other items I loved were the clam chowder, salmon, and omelete station mainly because they had guacamole as a filler choice. yum!   The rest of the food is pretty standard, but that was exactly what my husband was craving, so we were both happy.  I'm pregnant, so I didn't have the mimosa, but quite enjoyed their freshly squeezed orange juice.  My husband had a coffee and there was no extra charge for that either.

I'd definitely recommend sitting outside on the patio for the pretty view OR inside next to the dessert station so that you can get first dibs on a fresh plate of strawberries when they bring them out.  =)

Also, I was worried about having to wait a long time for a table since they don't take reservations, but we arrived a little before noon and were seated right away.  Nice job, Mr. Eastwood!

Passionfish  

Categories: Seafood, American (New)

4/25/2010
So I'm a seared-scallop fanatic, and always always always order it whenever it's on a menu.  With that being said, I was super excited to try both the appetizer and entree versions at Passionfish (This place has been bookmarked on my yelp account for awhile simply because of all the positive scallop reviews).  YES, the scallops were cooked perfectly... but I was not a fan of the accompaniments.  I've just had better pairings that's all.  I didn't care for either of the risotto preparations they came with... and I do love me some risotto!  I was bummed that I didn't love these dishes.

But what I did love was EVERYTHING else we had and I would definitely order them again.  Not only that, I would likely eat here again if I were in the area... I'd just skip out on the scallops (Seriously, I can't believe I'm saying that).  

The butternut squash soup was only $6 a bowl, and I practically licked that bowl clean.... ok I didn't, but I did grab a piece of bread and scraped the bowl w/ it... not w/o getting the evil-eye from my husband though.  

The halibut w/ prosciuto and some sort of breaded mushrooms 'n risotto was super good.  So good that when we got home 2 hours later, I killed my husband's leftovers.

The banana bread pudding w/ chocolate sauce for dessert was crazy good.  It has the perfect amount of sweet, savory, and a little crunch!   I too killed this leftover once I got home.

1 star for the halibut, 1 star for the butternut squash soup & 1 star for the banana bread pudding.
