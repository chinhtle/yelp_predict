Dal Toro Ristorante  

Category: Italian
Neighborhood: Eastside

7/21/2008
This place has a nice patio and summery cocktails which were perfect compliments to the scorching July weather.  

I stumbled upon this place as my friends and I were on our way out to dinner across the street at Social House.  We were approached by one of the staff members who asked us to check out their Happy Hour and grab a complimentary drink at the bar.  Hmmm... our reservations were for 8pm, and it was 7:55.... SURE, what the heck!  I ordered the Yellow Belly which I thoroughly enjoyed.  If you like lemonade, you'll LOVE this refreshing drink because it had lemoncello and ketel one citron.  

I liked the colorful display of red, orange, yellow and green Lamborghinis in the showroom.  (hehe... It reminded me of Skittles... they just need to add a purple one.)  

The place was a bit too stiff for my taste though, so I probably wouldn't go out of my way to come back and have dinner here.  Thanks for the free drinks though!!

Social House  

Categories: Japanese, Lounges, Asian Fusion
Neighborhood: The Strip

7/21/2008
Oh man did Social House deliver!!

This was the perfect place to eat with my 2 friends before hitting the town on a Saturday night.  We loved everything that we ordered, but the 2 stand outs were the yellowtail sashimi w/ tempura jalapenos on top and our chilled watermelon saki drinks.  I'm really not a huge fan of saki, but theirs tasted like fresh squeezed watermelon juice.. of which I AM a fan.  I also liked that they serve it chilled because warm saki kinda grosses me out.  This was also the 1st time I have ever tried yellowtail sashimi, as I usually stick to ahi tuna and salmon sashimi... but Social House has now broken my old sashimi ordering routine for good.  The other items we enjoyed were the miso cod, crab & shrimp fried rice, and lychee beigents.

Yes, the food is a bit pricey, but Social House is still 5 stars in my book cuz of the awesome staff who totally HOOKED US UP for the night w/ VIP services at the new Christian Audigier Nightclub below, as well as LAX and PURE.  So really, the price we paid for dinner was totally worth it!  And who doesn't love skipping long lines, no cover, and free drinks?  Go and have a ball... we definitely did!  Oh, and I'd also recommend sitting on the patio for a great view.

Thanks Social House!  I'll be back for more!!

Sino Restaurant & Lounge  

Categories: Chinese, Asian Fusion
Neighborhood: West San Jose

6/26/2008
It's ridiculous how much I love Sino's Char Siu Sea Bass.

I normally don't order sea bass whenever I see it on a menu because it tastes the same to me at every restaurant, and I always think that I can replicate it at home for much cheaper.  But Sino's sea bass is in a league of its own.  It has this smokey barbequed glaze on it that I wasn't expecting at all... but now that I've experienced the "char siu", I crave it like nobody's business.  Sigh... if only it weren't so expensive, I'd so have that at least once a week.  If anyone knows of a cheaper hole in the wall chinese spot that serves this, I'm all ears!

I would also recommend the crab risotto which complimented our sea bass perfectly.  For starters, the kobe beef sliders were pretty good.

I took 1 star away for the disappointing lychee sangrias that we ordered.  I was super excited when I saw this on their menu as those 2 ingredients are definitely huge favorites of mine.  Sadly, our watered-down pitcher had zero lychees and way too many lemons.  Conceptually, this drink sounds like a fabulous idea, but they failed miserably on the execution.

After dinner, we had to walk through the extremely crowded bar to get outta there.  The guys are pretty aggressive and all the girls seemed to be holding filled-to-the-rim martini glasses.  I thank the heavens that me and my very white gucci bag made it out safely.  =)  They do play good music, though.

Pampas  

Categories: Brazilian, Steakhouses

6/7/2008
I've been to Pampas 3 times now.   The 1st time, I had the Rodizio lunch buffet, and I fell in LOVE with everything about the place.  I couldn't get enough of their picahna, chicken thighs, grilled pineapples, and the bread and fried banana appetizers.  For $28 I thought it was a total STEAL.  It was a Wed. afternoon, and the place was not busy at all, so the service was great.  

My 2nd time was a bit of a disappointment.  I (along with 5 meat-loving guys) went for dinner on a Saturday night and had the Rodizio.  The service was slow, slow, SLOW and for $44, I didn't feel like I got my money's worth.  I was always waiting for the picahna to come back around, I'd finally get a thin slice that I'd gobble up in two bites, and then found myself waiting around again for the picahna to come back.  I'm probably too picky of an eater.  Once I taste something that I really like, I'll just want that over and over again.  I also stress myself out a tad during all-u-can-eat-style meals because I don't want to feel like I wasted my appetite and money by filling up on drinks, breads, rice, veggies, fruits... so this eating format may not be condusive for someone like me.

HOWEVER... my 3rd and most recent trip to Pampas was last night, where I opted to skip out on the Rodizio and order the ribeye steak.... ahhhhh... I finally found what I was looking for to satisfy all of my needs!!    I felt so much more relaxed because I didn't have to worry about pacing myself or trying to focus only on eating my favorite meats.  I ordered a really nice glass of Reisling white wine.  I ate fried bananas and dinner rolls to my heart's content.  I felt so relaxed cuz I knew that I'd be able to box up whatever I didn't finish!  So back to the ribeye... it was probably the best ribeye I've ever had!  It was cooked to perfection, very tasty, juicy, and came with these really yummy pearl onions that are soaked in some sort of vinegar.

So if you're like me and crave the meat, but probably can't eat $44 worth... I'd suggest either going during lunch hours during the weekday 4 the cheaper Rodizio, or ordering the $36 ribeye steakfor dinner.  Either way, your tummy and wallet will thank you!

Javier's Crystal Cove  

Category: Mexican

6/1/2008
2 words people: Enchiladas Poblanas.  Good God those chicken enchiladas covered in mole sauce were so freakin' tasty!   (I kid you not, the voice of Austin Powers kept ringing in my head after every bite: "MOOOOOOOOOOOOLE -- MOLE-MOLE-MOLE").  My friend ordered the Mole Poblano, and she loved her meal just as much.  

I also had a margarita on the rocks, and it was strong, sweet, salty and smooth all at the same time... PERFECTION.

We went on a Wednesday night during an NBA playoff game and it was packed.  We didn't have any reservations, but as soon as we got there we were seated at a booth that just opened up.  SWEET.  The service was great.  

My only negative was that I thought that the guacamole was overly spicy, and both of my friends agreed with me.  I liked that there wasn't too much (if any) cilantro in it cuz I'm one of those people who hates cilantro.  I just wish they would've asked us how spicy we'd like our guacamole prepared.

Overall, it's a great spot with great food and I'm for sure eating here again the next time I visit Orange County.

Hard Rock Hotel  

Category: Hotels

5/20/2008
... Just thought I'd share my experience and offer some tips.  I had a conference to go to at the Convention Center across the street (very convenient location by the way), so I booked a room for Saturday night.  The room was very cool with a plasma tv and an even cooler bathroom with an oh-so-heavenly double headed shower... one of them being a rainfall shower head... AAAAHHHH.  I always rate hotel rooms by their bathrooms, and this one's in my top 5.  They also let me do an early check in, which was very helpful.  

If you want to hit up the Sweetwater Saloon or Moonstone Lounge, then it definitely helps if you're a guest at the hotel.  But even if you ARE staying at the hotel, don't assume you'll automatically get in... make sure you go to the concierge desk and ask to be put on their guestlist.  (I actually saw some hotel guests being turned away.)  If you do that, then you'll have zero problems and will bypass the line of people outside.  My husband and I had fun at both places, but mainly because it was free and we didn't have to wait in line.  Otherwise, we probably wouldn't come back... cuz damn, those drinks were pricey!          

FYI: you can also use their concierge if you wanna be put on guestlists for other places too.  When I travel, I always utilize the concierge connections to get into hotspots... it totally works in Vegas too!
